About Me

Welcome! I am Dr. Meysam Motaharfar, a doctoral researcher at Louisiana State University. I was born in Qom, Iran, into a religious family. However, I quickly realized that religion could not answer the profound questions I had about the universe. This realization sparked my journey into the world of physics. I began my academic path by studying Physics at Semnan University in Iran. During my senior year, I discovered that cosmology might hold the key to addressing the questions that fascinated me. This epiphany marked the beginning of my specialization, as I pursued a master's degree in cosmology at Shahid Beheshti University. My passion for understanding the universe eventually led me to Louisiana State University, where I am now a doctoral researcher in theoretical cosmology and quantum gravity. my research focuses on the earliest moments of the universe, where the known laws of physics break down, necessitating a theory of quantum gravity. I'm deeply fascinated by how such theories could address primordial cosmological puzzles, including the universe's initial conditions, the formation of large-scale structures, and the mysteries of dark matter and dark energy.

Fascinated by the wide range of problems that data science and AI can tackle, I took the initiative to develop the necessary skills through self-study and hands-on projects. I am proficient in Python, along with key libraries such as NumPy, SciPY, Pandas, Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, and Matplotlib. My expertise spans Machine Learning algorithms, Deep Learning architectures, and data visualization techniques. Now, I am eager to transition into a data scientist role where I can leverage my quantitative and computational skills to create data-driven solutions in industry.
